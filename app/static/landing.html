Explore the connections between your ideas.

People don't think in paragraphs.

Thinktool is an alternative to note-taking tools and outliners like Evernote and Workflowy that works in a different way.

Thinktool looks like an outliner, but underneath it structures your items like a graph. Rather than arranging items in a tree, you connect related items to each other. If you want to, you can then structure those items into loose hierarchies, but the same item may be in multiple places and items can even be their own parents. This model gives you the freedom to write down your ideas in the same way they are structured in your mind.

Give it a try.

I'm currently working on developing Thinktool. However, you can try it out right now, by signing up. Please be aware that Thinktool is very unstable, and since the current preview is only intended for testing, your data may be deleted at any time.

Alternatives to Thinktool.

Roam is a fairly new app that does many of the same things as Thinktool, but which does most of them better. I'd recommend checking it out!

Thinktool is directly inspired by outliners like Org mode, Workflowy and Dynalist, though Thinktool uses a more powerful data model underneath.

Thinktool stores its data in a similar way to TheBrain, but wheraas Thinktool presents its data like a tree, TheBrain tries to look more like a mind-mapping tool.
